Reading The Orbs — 02

One glance, four reads.

DS Systems plants glowing circular orbs on the chart. They share one visual language — a translucent core wrapped in glow rings that grow with conviction. Bigger and brighter means a stronger signal. Color tells you which system spoke and what it means.

Green orb — a long idea

Pink orb — a short idea

Purple orb — an opening-range breakout

DS Orbit stamps the point where price broke the session's opening range. A confirmed, explosive break earns a full circled halo; a weak one stays a bare core.Source · DS Orbit

Amber orb — a mean-reversion snap-back

A big, bright, double-ringed orb is a live, high-conviction call. A small, faint one is a soft or historical note. The orb does the reading — the final click is always yours.
Zones That Matter — 03

DS Orbit

Orbit is the backbone — the publisher every other registry system reads. It aggregates support & resistance, order blocks, opening-range breakouts and volume-at-price across multiple timeframes, scores each level for confidence, merges the overlaps, and publishes one clean, immutable map. Untested levels start humble and only earn their rating through real interaction — and the moment a level breaks, it's gone. When a level flips its role, Orbit flips with it.

The Signature Heat Map — 04

DS Ember

Ember watches the engine of the trend and colors every bar to match. Young trends burn bright and clean; as a move runs out of fuel it cools to a dark, smoky exhaust — the early tell that a trend is about to refuel or turn. When a run stretches beyond its statistical limit, Ember grays it out: the turn is near. And it does it with zero repaints.

Premium & Discount — 05

DS Balance

Balance answers the only question that matters at the moment of entry: is price rich or cheap? A session-anchored runway maps Premium, Equilibrium and Discount in real time, built from session VWAP and its volatility bands. A bold readout calls exactly where the market sits — and how far — so you can buy the discount, fade the premium, and never get long at the top by accident.

Conviction, On The Chart — 06

DS Stars

Stars turn Orbit's map into an actual idea. When one of three confirmed setups completes — a sweep reversal, an order-block continuation, or an opening-range breakout — Stars plants a single orb: green for a long, pink for a short. The orb grows and brightens with the model's conviction — its "stars" — so a big, double-ringed orb is a high-conviction live call and a small faint one is a weak or historical note.

Order Flow — 08

DS Pulse

Pulse is the standalone member of the suite — it reveals the footprints of the players who actually move price. Every aggressive block that hits the tape lands as a glowing print, larger when the size is larger, colored by who's leaning on the market. It runs on its own private one-tick series — not the DS Registry, and without forcing the platform-wide Tick Replay that bogs a chart down. The quiet before a move isn't quiet at all: you see the heavy hands step in.
