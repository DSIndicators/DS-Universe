What The Radars Do — 01

The tape talks.
The Radars translate.

DS Radars are live read-outs that turn raw order flow into a plain-language verdict. Three instruments work the same chart from different angles — Pilots reads direction and conviction, Sweeper hunts traps and liquidity, Everguard grades the integrity of every move. Each is a self-contained cockpit, cross-referenced across five timeframes and rendered without a hint of lag — so the read is always current and always trustworthy.

The Trap Hunter — 03

DS Sweeper

Codename Hunta. Sweeper watches for the moves that fool everyone else — sweeps, stop-hunts, absorption, fake breakouts and weak continuations. It spots them the way the desks do: price pierces a recent swing, then closes back inside on heavy volume. A Threat Index and a Conviction bar quantify the danger, while a plain status line calls it for what it is. When Hunta says the trap is set, you see it before it springs — and you know when strength is really fatigue.

Integrity & Conviction — 04

DS Everguard

Everguard grades trust. It scores Signal Integrity and Candle Conviction in real time, then lines up Main-Time-Frame Strength from the 1-minute to the 4-hour so you can see, at a glance, whether the whole board agrees. Volume Strength, Volatility and a Middle-Grounds bull-vs-bear tug round out the picture, with a Next-Bar timer keeping you in rhythm. Built-in "stickiness" stops colors flickering on a threshold, and a demotion matrix quietly downgrades a bright trend the moment volume or continuity weakens. It answers the only question that matters: can I trust this move?
