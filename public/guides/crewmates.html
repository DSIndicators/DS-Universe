Break Of Structure & Change Of Character — 02

DS BC

DS BC reads the chart's skeleton for you. It marks BOS when a trend pushes through its last swing and keeps going, CHoCH the moment momentum flips the other way, and the EQH / EQL liquidity pools price loves to hunt. Bullish events glow green, bearish events glow orange — so the story of the move is readable at a glance.

Trend Line — 03

DS TL

DS TL draws one clean trend line — the one that actually matters right now. In an uptrend it tracks ascending support in green; in a downtrend it tracks descending resistance in orange; sideways, it greys out. Each new swing refreshes it to the freshest pivots, a retroactive check rejects any line that intervening candles already pierced, and the instant price breaks or drifts away from it, the line deletes itself. You never stare at a stale line again.

DS TL · Self-Managing Trend LineGreen up · Orange down · Grey flat

Always the right line

No drawing, no maintenance. DS TL re-anchors to the current trend so the line on screen is the live one.

Validated retroactively

A line that intervening candles already cut through is rejected — only trustworthy diagonals survive.

Never goes stale

Break it or abandon it and it's gone — not greyed, not frozen. The chart stays honest about the trend.

Projects a runway

The line extends ahead of price, giving you a forward edge to plan entries, targets and invalidations against.

How to read it
Green line

Ascending support — higher lows, uptrend.

Orange line

Descending resistance — lower highs, downtrend.

Grey line

Flat / sideways — no committed trend.

Support & Resistance — 04

DS SR

DS SR finds the levels that earn their keep. Confirmed swing pivots are clustered into touch-weighted zones and ranked by strength — S ×1, S ×2… for support, R ×1, R ×2… for resistance — so the more a level is respected, the louder it speaks. Support glows green, resistance orange, and when price decisively closes through a level, DS SR erases it. The chart cleans itself after every sweep.

DS SR · Touch-Ranked LevelsThey're not just random lines

Strength you can read

The ×-count tells you how many times a level held. Trade the strong ones, ignore the noise.

Self-cleaning rails

Break a level and it disappears, so you're never anchored to a zone the market already gave up on.

Touch-weighted clustering

Nearby pivots merge into one honest level — the more it's tested, the heavier it reads.

Clean projected zones

Levels extend neatly to the right of price as runways — clear targets and decision points, no clutter.

How to read it
Green zone

Support — a floor price has respected.

Orange zone

Resistance — a ceiling price has respected.

×-count

How many times the level held — its strength.
